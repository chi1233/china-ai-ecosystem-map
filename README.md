# China AI Ecosystem — Top 40 Startups

**Capstone Research Visualization**  
Chinonso Anyanwu · Schwarzman College, Tsinghua University · 2026

---

## Overview

An interactive, single-page web application mapping China's **top 40 AI startups** against the three domains of *developmental orchestration* — the central analytical framework of this capstone research.

The map visualizes how China's **AI Pilot Zone** program coordinates capital, data infrastructure, and institutional integration to produce frontier AI ecosystems across Beijing, Hangzhou, Shanghai, and Shenzhen.

---

## Research Framework

This visualization is built around the capstone's core argument:

> China's AI Pilot Zones represent a new form of **digital-era state capacity** — exercised not through direct ownership or command, but through *developmental orchestration*: coordinating capital allocation, governing data and compute infrastructure, and integrating universities, startups, and government agencies into shared innovation platforms.

### Three Orchestration Domains

| Domain | Description | Key Actors |
|---|---|---|
| **Capital Coordination** | State guidance funds co-investing with private VCs at strategic horizons | Beijing State Capital Fund, municipal AI funds |
| **Data & Compute Governance** | Subsidized access to compute; structured data-sharing frameworks | Huawei Cloud, Alibaba Cloud, BAAI |
| **Institutional Integration** | University-industry spinouts, joint labs, talent circulation pipelines | Tsinghua KEG → Zhipu AI; TU/CMU → Moonshot AI |

## Features

- **40 companies** across Foundation Models, Cloud/Compute, AI Chips, Robotics, Applications, and Research Infrastructure
- **Interactive filters** by sector (LLM, Cloud, Chips, Robotics, Apps, Infra)
- **City breakdown panel** showing AI Pilot Zone concentration
- **Research insights** cards linking ecosystem data to capstone arguments
- **Capstone relevance scores** (1–5) for each company
- **Light / Dark mode toggle**
- Fully responsive — works on desktop and mobile
