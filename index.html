Research Findings

Key Structural Insights

01

Beijing–Shanghai Axis

Over 60% of top-40 companies cluster in Beijing and Shanghai, correlating directly with the density of state-backed AI Pilot Zone designations and top-tier university spinout ecosystems.

02

Foundation Model Race

Since 2023, at least 11 Chinese startups have launched competing large language models, each anchored to a different pilot zone and receiving differentiated state procurement guarantees.

03

Developmental Orchestration

MIIT and NDRC jointly designate zone membership, enabling startups to access preferential land, compute subsidies, and fast-track data-sharing agreements unavailable outside zones.

04

Chip Sovereignty Imperative

U.S. export controls post-2022 turbocharged domestic chip startups. Cambricon, Biren, and Moore Threads each received multi-billion RMB guidance-fund injections within 18 months of sanctions.

05

Healthcare AI as Policy Priority

The "Healthy China 2030" mandate drives disproportionate state procurement for healthcare AI firms, creating a captive first-customer market that de-risks early commercialisation.

06

Public–Private Partnership Structure

Unlike Silicon Valley's arms-length model, China's leading AI startups typically carry a state-linked anchor investor, a pilot zone designation, and a government data-access agreement simultaneously.
